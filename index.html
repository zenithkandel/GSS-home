The Challenge

Why Remote Nepal Needs This

Over 3.5 million people in Nepal's mountain regions face life-threatening delays in emergency response due to infrastructure limitations.

No Cellular Coverage

Remote Himalayan villages lack mobile network infrastructure. A medical emergency can't even make a call.

40% of Nepal lacks coverage

Frequent Power Outages

Grid electricity is unreliable or non-existent. Villages can go days without power during emergencies.

18hrs average daily outage

Critical Delays

Without communication, rescue teams don't know an emergency exists until it's often too late.

4-8hrs typical response delay

Natural Disasters

Earthquakes, floods, and landslides destroy existing infrastructure instantly, isolating entire regions.

Annual disaster events
3.5M+

People in Nepal's remote regions lack reliable emergency communication infrastructure

The Solution

How LifeLine Works

A mesh network of low-power devices that relay emergency signals across mountains—no cellular towers or internet required.

01

Emergency Triggered

User presses SOS button or AI detects emergency via sensors (fall detection, vitals, etc.)

02

Edge Processing

ESP32-S3 processes data locally with TensorFlow Lite, classifies emergency type and priority.

03

LoRa Mesh Relay

Signal hops through mesh network, each node extending range by 10+ km across terrain.

04

Gateway Received

Gateway station with internet connectivity receives signal and syncs with cloud dashboard.

05

Rescue Dispatched

Operators view real-time alerts on dashboard, dispatch nearest appropriate responders.
